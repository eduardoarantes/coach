Recovery data tells you when to push and when to back off. Coach Watts aggregates wellness metrics from your connected devices and combines them with subjective check-ins for a complete readiness picture.

## Where to find recovery data

| Page                 | What it shows                                   |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Today's Wellness** | Daily HRV, sleep, resting HR, recovery scores   |
| **Morning Check-in** | Subjective feel — soreness, energy, mood        |
| **Dashboard**        | Recovery summary widget alongside training load |
| **Recommendations**  | How recovery factors into today's guidance      |

## Key recovery metrics

| Metric                       | Source                    | Used for                           |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **HRV**                      | Garmin, Oura, WHOOP       | Autonomic nervous system readiness |
| **Sleep duration & quality** | All wellness integrations | Recovery capacity assessment       |
| **Resting heart rate**       | Wearables                 | Fatigue and illness detection      |
| **Recovery score**           | WHOOP, Oura readiness     | Single-number readiness indicator  |
| **Subjective wellness**      | Morning Check-in          | Context devices cannot capture     |

## Device-specific recovery

Different devices contribute different signals:

- **[Garmin](/documentation/athletes/integrations/garmin)** — overnight HRV, sleep stages, Body Battery, stress
- **[Oura](/documentation/athletes/integrations/oura)** — readiness score, sleep score, temperature deviation
- **[WHOOP](/documentation/athletes/integrations/whoop)** — recovery %, strain, sleep performance
- **[Withings](/documentation/athletes/integrations/withings)** — weight trends affecting readiness context

Connect at least one wellness source for recovery-aware recommendations.

## Morning check-in

The morning check-in captures what devices miss:

1. Open **Morning Check-in** from the sidebar
2. Rate sleep quality, energy, and muscle soreness
3. Add notes about stress, travel, or illness
4. Submit — the AI factors this into today's [recommendation](/documentation/athletes/recommendations)

::alert{type="tip"}
A bad subjective check-in combined with poor HRV is a strong signal to ease off — even if you feel obligated to complete a hard session.
::

## Recovery in AI coaching

Recovery data influences:

- **Daily recommendations** — proceed, modify, or rest
- **Training plan adaptation** — block intensity adjustments
- **Nutrition targets** — recovery days shift to lower carb emphasis
- **Chat responses** — the coach references your latest biometrics

Example prompts:

- _"Why is my recovery score low this week?"_
- _"Show my HRV trend over the last 14 days."_
- _"Should I train today based on my sleep and HRV?"_

## Recovery Capacity score

Coach Watts generates a **Recovery Capacity** athlete profile score (1–10) based on how well your body adapts to training stress over time. Find it on your athlete profile alongside Aerobic Capacity and Consistency scores.
