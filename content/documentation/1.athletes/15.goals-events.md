Goals give your training direction. Coach Watts uses goals to generate [training plans](/documentation/athletes/training-plans), prioritize AI recommendations, and measure progress.

## Goal types

Create goals at **Profile → Goals**:

| Type                  | Examples                                           |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Body composition**  | Reach 72 kg by March, reduce body fat to 12%       |
| **Event preparation** | Complete a century ride in June, marathon sub-3:30 |
| **Performance**       | Increase FTP to 280W, improve 10K pace to 4:30/km  |
| **Consistency**       | Train 5 days per week for 8 weeks                  |

Each goal includes a title, target value, optional deadline, and priority level.

## Creating a goal

1. Go to **Profile → Goals**
2. Click **Add Goal**
3. Select the goal type
4. Enter target metrics and dates
5. Save — the AI incorporates the goal into coaching context

The AI may also **suggest goals** based on your training history and recent performance trends.

## Events

The **Events** page tracks races and key dates:

- Event name, date, and priority (A, B, or C race)
- Distance, elevation, and terrain profile
- Link to an active training plan

Events anchor plan generation — the AI structures mesocycles to peak at your A-race date.

### Adding an event

1. Open **Events** from the sidebar
2. Click **Add Event**
3. Enter race details and priority
4. Optionally link to a [training plan](/documentation/athletes/training-plans)

Events can also sync from [Intervals.icu](/documentation/athletes/integrations/intervals-icu) or [Strava](/documentation/athletes/integrations/strava) if your calendar is connected.

## Goal-driven training

When you create a plan through the Plan Wizard, you select a goal that drives:

- Block length and focus (base, build, peak, taper)
- Weekly volume progression
- Intensity distribution strategy
- Taper timing before event date

## Tracking progress

Goal cards show:

- Current vs. target values
- Progress bar (for measurable metrics like weight)
- Status (active, completed, archived)
- Priority badge

Ask Chat about progress:

- _"How am I tracking toward my FTP goal?"_
- _"How many weeks until my A-race?"_
